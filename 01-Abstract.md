Outpatient operations involve departments, doctors, patients, appointments, schedules, medical records, and prescriptions, and information flows frequently across roles; however, management that relies on paper registration or fragmented systems often leads to inconsistent data standards, low retrieval efficiency, fragmented processes, and lagging coordination, making cross-department workflows hard to close in a loop, while the absence of unified permissions and data standards constrains continuous management of appointments, diagnosis, and medication as well as statistical analysis and quality traceability in day-to-day outpatient services. This study targets outpatient workflows in medical institutions and designs a front-end/back-end separated medical information system: the front end uses Vue 3, Vite, and Element Plus to build business interfaces, and the back end is built on Spring Boot 3.2, MyBatis-Plus, Spring Security, JWT, and MySQL to provide a unified service layer that covers users and permissions, departments, doctors, patients, appointments, schedules, medical records, medicines, and prescriptions; it forms a closed loop around appointment requests, schedule maintenance, medical record entry, prescription issuance, and medicine circulation, and by adopting a unified data model and API conventions it improves consistency, traceability, and maintenance efficiency, supporting higher outpatient service efficiency, medication safety control, statistical reporting, and management decision making in routine operations. Keywords: Medical information system; outpatient management; prescription management; Spring Boot
